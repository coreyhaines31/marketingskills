Key Takeaways

  • Morpho delivers ~46% higher yield on USDC lending (5.87% vs 4.01%) through curator-optimized market allocation.
  • Morpho's collateral is 100% blue chip — only BTC and ETH derivatives (cbBTC, WBTC, wstETH, WETH). No synthetic or algorithmic assets.
  • Aave carries ~13.5% USDe (Ethena) exposure — a synthetic dollar with delta-neutral hedging risk that Chaos Labs has flagged as a potential "liquidity squeeze" concern at $6.6B.
  • Morpho uses isolated markets — bad debt in one collateral market (e.g., WBTC depeg) cannot infect other markets. In Aave's shared pool, a bad debt event from any collateral type affects all USDC suppliers.
  • Aave has 19x more TVL ($6.13B vs $316M), offering deeper liquidity but also greater systemic exposure to 20+ collateral types including restaked and synthetic assets.
