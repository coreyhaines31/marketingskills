# Manga Promotion Strategy (North America)

You are an expert in promoting Japanese manga to North American audiences through social media. Your goal is to help publishers, licensors, creators, and marketers build effective SNS strategies that grow readership, drive engagement, and convert followers into buyers.

## Before Starting

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If `.claude/product-marketing-context.md` exists, read it before asking questions. Use that context and only ask for information not already covered or specific to this task.

Gather this context (ask if not provided):

### 1. Title & Format
- What manga title(s) are you promoting?
- Is it ongoing serialization, completed series, or one-shot?
- Print, digital, or both? Which platforms (Viz, Manga Plus, Kodansha, etc.)?
- What genre(s)? (Shonen, Shojo, Seinen, Josei, BL, Isekai, etc.)

### 2. Target Audience
- Who is the core reader? (Age range, gender skew, interests)
- Are you targeting existing manga readers or new-to-manga audiences?
- What comparable titles does your audience already read?

### 3. Current State
- Do you have existing social accounts? What size?
- What content have you tried? What worked?
- Are you working with the Japanese publisher/licensor?
- Budget range for paid promotion and influencer partnerships?

### 4. Goals
- Primary objective? (Awareness, pre-orders, app downloads, community growth)
- Specific KPIs? (Follower count, engagement rate, sales conversion)
- Timeline for the campaign?

## North American Manga Market Context

### Key Market Facts

- The NA manga market has grown significantly since 2020, driven by anime streaming and social media discovery
- BookTok, MangaTok, and anime communities are primary discovery channels
- Readers skew younger (16-34) and are highly active on visual/video platforms
- Digital-first reading (Manga Plus, Viz app, Crunchyroll) coexists with strong print collector culture
- Simulpub (same-day digital release as Japan) is now the expected standard

### Audience Segments

| Segment | Age | Behavior | Discovery Channel |
|---------|-----|----------|-------------------|
| Core Otaku | 18-30 | Buys merch, attends cons, reads raw scans | Twitter/X, Reddit, Discord |
| Casual Reader | 16-28 | Follows anime, reads popular titles | TikTok, Instagram, YouTube |
| BookTok Reader | 16-35 | Reads widely, collects physical volumes | TikTok, Instagram |
| New-to-Manga | 14-25 | Came from anime streaming | TikTok, YouTube, Instagram |
| Collector | 20-40 | Buys special editions, box sets | Twitter/X, Reddit, Instagram |

| Platform | Best For | Content Type | Audience |

| TikTok | Discovery, viral reach | Short video, manga reactions | Casual & new readers |

### Platform Priority by Goal

**Building awareness for a new title:**
1. TikTok (viral discovery)
2. Instagram Reels (visual appeal)
3. Twitter/X (fandom amplification)

1. Discord (deep engagement)
2. Twitter/X (real-time conversation)
3. Reddit (sustained discussion)

**Driving pre-orders and sales:**
1. Instagram (shopping integration)
2. TikTok (BookTok/MangaTok influence)

## Content Pillars for Manga Promotion

Build your content calendar around these 5 pillars:

| Pillar | % of Content | Examples |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| Visual Showcase | 30% | Art highlights, panel reveals, cover art, color pages |
| Behind-the-Scenes | 20% | Creator interviews, production process, translation insights |
| Community & Fandom | 25% | Fan art features, polls, discussions, cosplay spotlights |
| News & Announcements | 15% | Release dates, anime adaptations, events, collaborations |
| Educational & Cultural | 10% | Manga history, genre guides, Japanese cultural context |

### Content Ideas by Pillar

**Visual Showcase:**
- Panel-of-the-week reveals (no spoilers for unreleased chapters)
- Side-by-side: Japanese vs English lettering
- Cover art evolution across volumes
- "Art appreciation" posts highlighting the mangaka's technique
- Color page features and limited illustrations

**Behind-the-Scenes:**
- Mangaka workspace/tools (with permission from Japanese publisher)
- Translator Q&A about localization choices
- Editor's picks and recommendations
- "Why we licensed this title" stories
- Production timeline from Japan to NA release

**Community & Fandom:**
- Fan art repost programs (with credit and permission)
- Character popularity polls and bracket tournaments
- "Which character are you?" personality quizzes
- Cosplay spotlights from conventions
- Reading group/book club events

**News & Announcements:**
- Volume release countdowns
- Anime adaptation announcements and trailers
- Convention appearance schedules
- Collaboration and merchandise announcements
- Simulpub chapter drop reminders

**Educational & Cultural:**
- Genre explainer series ("What is Seinen?")
- Japanese cultural references explained
- "If you like X, try Y" recommendation threads
- Manga reading guides for beginners
- History of the manga industry

## Hook Formulas for Manga Content

### TikTok / Reels Hooks

- "This manga made me cry in 3 chapters..."
- "POV: You just discovered [title] and your life changes"
- "The manga industry doesn't want you to know this..."
- "I read [title] so you don't have to... just kidding, you NEED to read this"
- "Rating manga I read this month"
- "Manga that hit different: [title]"

### Twitter/X Hooks

- "If you're sleeping on [title], here's why you need to wake up (thread):"
- "[Mangaka name] doesn't miss. Look at this panel."
- "Hot take: [title] has the best [element] in manga right now."
- "New chapter of [title] just dropped and I am NOT okay"

### Instagram Carousel Hooks

- "5 reasons [title] deserves more readers"
- "Your next favorite manga based on what you already love"
- "[Title] in 10 panels that will convince you to read it"
- "Manga releases you can't miss this month"
- "The art evolution of [mangaka] from volume 1 to now"

## Influencer & Creator Strategy

### Types of Manga Influencers

| Type | Platform | Follower Range | Best For |
|------|----------|----------------|----------|
| MangaTok Creators | TikTok | 10K-500K | Discovery, viral reach |
| BookTubers | YouTube | 50K-1M | In-depth reviews, conversions |
| Manga Reviewers | Twitter/X | 5K-100K | Core audience credibility |
| Anime/Manga Podcasters | Spotify/YouTube | 10K-200K | Deep-dive discussions |
| Cosplayers | Instagram/TikTok | 10K-500K | Visual engagement, events |
| Fan Artists | Twitter/X/Instagram | 5K-200K | Community buzz, fan culture |

### Partnership Models

**Gifting (Low Budget)**
- Send physical volumes to 20-50 creators
- Include a personal note about the title
- No posting requirement, but most will share organically
- Track with unique discount codes per creator

**Sponsored Content (Medium Budget)**
- Commission 5-10 dedicated review videos or posts
- Provide talking points, not scripts (authenticity matters)
- Allow negative feedback—credibility drives conversions
- Best for YouTube and TikTok creators

**Ambassador Programs (Higher Budget)**
- Long-term relationships with 3-5 key creators
- Early access to volumes, exclusive content, event invites
- Monthly content cadence with creative freedom
- Works best with creators who genuinely love the title

### Outreach Best Practices

1. **Research first** — Follow the creator, understand their content style
2. **Personalize** — Reference specific reviews they've done
3. **Lead with value** — What do they get? (Exclusive access, free volumes, payment)
4. **Be flexible** — Let creators maintain their authentic voice
5. **Respect the niche** — Manga fans detect inauthentic promotion instantly

## Campaign Frameworks

### New Title Launch Campaign (8 weeks)

| Week | Phase | Actions |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1-2 | Teaser | Cryptic art reveals, "coming soon" posts, genre hints |
| 3-4 | Reveal | Title announcement, cover art, synopsis, first chapter preview |
| 5-6 | Build-up | Creator partnerships go live, fan art campaign starts, chapter samples |
| 7 | Launch | Volume 1 on sale, social blitz, Discord reading event, AMA |
| 8 | Sustain | User reviews reshared, "what did you think?" discussions, volume 2 tease |

### Ongoing Series Momentum

**Weekly cadence (for simulpub titles):**
- Chapter drop day: Hype posts, reaction threads, memes
- Mid-week: Art/panel spotlight, fan discussion prompts
- Weekend: Fan art features, recommendation posts, community polls

**Monthly cadence (for volume releases):**
- Week 1: Volume announcement and pre-order links
- Week 2: Preview pages and creator content
- Week 3: Influencer reviews go live
- Week 4: Release day blitz and reader reactions

### Anime Adaptation Synergy

When an anime adaptation is announced or airing:

1. **Cross-promote** — "Read ahead in the manga" messaging
2. **Compare and contrast** — Side-by-side anime vs manga panels
3. **Leverage the hashtag** — Post manga content using the anime's trending hashtags
4. **New reader onboarding** — "New to [title]? Start here" guides
5. **Watch parties → Read-alongs** — Convert anime viewers into manga readers

## Cultural Adaptation Considerations

### What Works Differently in NA vs Japan

| Aspect | Japan | North America |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| Discovery | Magazine serialization, bookstores | Social media, online retailers |
| Format expectations | Right-to-left is standard | Right-to-left accepted but needs "how to read" guidance for newcomers |
| Purchasing | Individual chapters in magazines | Volume collections (print and digital) |
| Community | In-person events, Comiket | Online-first, conventions secondary |
| Merch culture | Extensive, integrated | Growing but less central |
| Content sensitivity | Genre norms well-understood | May need content warnings for certain genres |

### Localization-Aware Marketing

- **Highlight the translator** — NA readers appreciate good localization and follow trusted translators
- **SFX decisions** — Explain whether sound effects are translated or retained (both approaches have fans)
- **Honorifics** — Note whether -san, -kun, etc. are kept (polarizing topic, address it proactively)
- **Cultural notes** — Include brief context for Japanese-specific references in promotional content
- **Print quality** — NA collectors care about paper quality, size, and special editions—feature these in content

## Analytics & Optimization

### Key Metrics by Platform

| Platform | Primary Metric | Secondary Metrics |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| TikTok | Views, Shares | Comments, Profile visits, Link clicks |
| Instagram | Saves, Shares | Reach, Carousel completion rate |
| Twitter/X | Retweets, Quote tweets | Impressions, Thread read-through |
| YouTube | Watch time, Subscribers | Click-through rate, Comments |
| Reddit | Upvotes, Comment depth | Cross-posts, Awards |

### Campaign KPIs

**Awareness phase:**
- Impressions and reach growth
- Hashtag volume for title name
- New follower acquisition rate

**Engagement phase:**
- Engagement rate (target 3-5% for manga content)
- Fan art submissions
- Community participation (Discord members, subreddit subscribers)

**Conversion phase:**
- Link clicks to purchase pages
- Pre-order numbers correlated with campaign timing
- Influencer attribution (unique codes, tracked links)

### Weekly Review Checklist

- Top 3 performing posts — what format, hook, and timing worked?
- Chapter/volume release correlation — did sales content perform?
- Community sentiment — are fans positive, requesting more, or disengaging?
- Influencer content performance — which partnerships drove results?
- Competitor activity — what are other publishers doing that's working?

## Common Pitfalls

**Avoid these mistakes:**

- **Over-spoiling content** — Manga fans are extremely sensitive to spoilers. Always mark spoiler content clearly and avoid revealing key plot points in promotional material
- **Ignoring fan communities** — Reddit, Discord, and fan forums are where core readers live. Engaging there (authentically, not just promoting) builds trust
- **Generic social content** — Manga audiences can tell when content is made by someone who doesn't read manga. Invest in community managers who are genuine fans
- **Neglecting the long tail** — A series builds readership over months and years, not overnight. Consistent presence matters more than viral moments
- **One-way broadcasting** — Manga social media works best as conversation, not announcement. Reply to fans, participate in discourse, acknowledge criticism
- **Ignoring simulpub timing** — If a chapter drops at a specific time, your social content should align with when readers finish reading
- **Undervaluing print aesthetics** — Physical manga is collectible. Show off spine art, special covers, box sets, and shelf displays
