# Email Templates — Valuation Outreach

Copy-paste templates for each stage of the outreach sequence. Customize the bracketed fields for each prospect.

---

## Email #1: The Opener

### Template A: Industry Map Frame (Recommended)

**Subject:** `{Company Name} — agency valuation`

```
Hi {First Name},

We're mapping the digital agency landscape — analyzing revenue
potential, service mix, and market positioning across {number}
agencies in {niche/region}.

{Company Name} came up in our research. Based on what we found,
your agency has some interesting strengths that most in your
space don't.

We put together a valuation snapshot for {Company Name}. Want
to see it?

{Your Name}
```

**Why it works:** Research frame, specific to them, teases a positive finding, easy yes/no question, no link.

---

### Template B: Benchmark Study Frame

**Subject:** `How {Company Name} compares`

```
Hi {First Name},

We just finished benchmarking {number} {business type} on
{key metrics — e.g., "pricing, positioning, and estimated
revenue"}.

{Company Name} was included. You {scored/ranked} {teaser —
e.g., "higher than most agencies your size on service
differentiation"}.

Full results are ready if you want them.

{Your Name}
```

---

### Template C: Specific Detail Opener

**Subject:** `{Company Name} — interesting finding`

```
Hi {First Name},

I was looking at {Company Name}'s site — {specific detail,
e.g., "the way you've positioned your SEO services alongside
content strategy is unusual in a good way"}.

We've been analyzing {number} agencies in {space} and putting
together valuations for each. Yours is done.

Curious to see where {Company Name} landed?

{Your Name}
```

**Why it works:** The specific website detail proves this isn't a mass blast. It shows you actually looked.

---

### Template D: Minimal / Ultra-Short

**Subject:** `{Company Name} valuation`

```
Hi {First Name},

We valued {Company Name} as part of a {industry} agency
research project. Your results are ready.

Want me to send them over?

{Your Name}
```

**Why it works:** Some founders prefer brevity. This respects their time and creates curiosity through what it doesn't say.

## Email #3: The Final Nudge (5-7 days after Email #2, only if no reply)

### Template A: Graceful Close

**Subject:** `Re: {Company Name} — agency valuation`

```
Hi {First Name},

Last note on this — I'll close out {Company Name}'s
valuation file at the end of {timeframe, e.g., "this
week" or "this batch"}.

If you'd like the results, just reply "send it" and I'll
get them to you. If not, no worries at all.

{Your Name}
```

**Why it works:** Creates soft urgency without being pushy. "No worries" gives them permission to ignore it, which paradoxically makes them more likely to respond.

## Reply Templates

### When They Say "Yes, Send It"

```
Great — here's your report:

{link}

You'll see your overall valuation up top, then a breakdown
of how we got there (service mix, positioning, market
comparables).

{Optional: "One thing that stood out — {specific insight}."}

Let me know what you think.

{Your Name}
```

---

### When They Say "What Is This?" / "Who Are You?"

```
Fair question — {Your Name} from {Your Company/Project}.

We're {one-sentence description of project, e.g., "building
a comprehensive map of the digital agency market — analyzing
positioning, services, and estimated valuations"}.

We included {Company Name} based on {how you found them —
e.g., "your presence in the {niche} space"}. The analysis
is based on public information — your website, service
offerings, market positioning, and industry benchmarks.

No cost, no obligation. Just thought the results might be
interesting to you as the owner.

Want me to send your report?

{Your Name}
```

**Key:** Be transparent. Vagueness here kills trust.

---

### When They Say "Not Interested"

```
No problem at all. Thanks for letting me know.

If you ever want to see the results down the road, just
reply to this thread.

{Your Name}
```

**Keep it short. Don't try to convince them.**

---

### When They Say "How Did You Get My Email?"

```
We sourced it from {honest answer — e.g., "your company
website" or "LinkedIn" or "a public agency directory"}.

We're reaching out to agency founders whose companies
appeared in our research. Happy to remove you from our
list — just say the word.

{Your Name}
```

**Never lie about sourcing. Transparency builds trust even when they're skeptical.**

---

### When They Say "This Is Spam"

```
Apologies for the unwanted email. Removing you from our
list now. You won't hear from us again.

{Your Name}
```

**Don't argue. Don't explain. Just remove them.**

## Post-Claim Follow-Up (24-48 hours after they view the report)

### Template A: The Insight Follow-Up

```
Hi {First Name},

Hope the report was useful. One thing that stood out
about {Company Name} — {specific insight from their
valuation, e.g., "your recurring revenue ratio is
strong, but there might be room to increase average
contract value"}.

Worth a quick conversation if you want to dig into
what's behind the numbers. {CTA — e.g., "Here's my
calendar: {link}" or "Want me to send over a detailed
breakdown?"}.

Either way, the report is yours to keep.

{Your Name}
```

---

### Template B: The Comparison Follow-Up

```
Hi {First Name},

Quick note after your report — {Company Name} {comparison
insight, e.g., "is in the top 30% of agencies we've
analyzed on service differentiation, but below average
on estimated margins"}.

That gap usually means {brief explanation of what it
means and why it matters}.

Happy to walk through it if useful — {CTA}.

{Your Name}
```

---

### Template C: The Direct Ask

```
Hi {First Name},

Now that you've seen where {Company Name} stands — curious
if you'd want to explore what it would take to {desired
outcome, e.g., "move the valuation number" or "close the
gap on pricing"}.

{CTA — e.g., "I have a few slots this week: {link}"}

No pressure either way.

{Your Name}
```

## Batch Management Notes

### For a 10-Agency Batch

**Day 1:** Send Email #1 to all 10 (spread across the morning, not all at once)
**Day 1-4:** Monitor for replies. Respond to any within hours.
**Day 4-5:** Send Email #2 to non-responders
**Day 5-9:** Monitor for replies
**Day 10-12:** Send Email #3 (final) to remaining non-responders
**Day 12+:** Close out non-responders. Move to next batch.
