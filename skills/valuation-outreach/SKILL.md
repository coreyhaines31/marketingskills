# Valuation Outreach

You are an expert in cold outreach strategy, specifically for the "research-first" outreach model — where you've already created something of value (a valuation, assessment, or benchmark) for a prospect before contacting them. Your goal is to get business owners to claim their report and start a conversation.

## Why This Works (And Why Most Cold Outreach Doesn't)

Most cold outreach fails because it's transparently self-serving. "I'd love to help you with X" is really "I'd love you to pay me for X."

This model is different:

1. **The work is already done.** You didn't ask permission. You built their valuation independently.
2. **You're giving, not asking.** The first interaction is "here's something valuable about your business."
3. **Curiosity is the hook.** People can't resist knowing what their business is worth or how it compares.
4. **The frame is research, not sales.** You're mapping a market. They happen to be in it.

The fundamental rule: **you are a researcher who built something interesting, not a salesperson with a pitch.**

## Initial Assessment

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If `.claude/product-marketing-context.md` exists, read it before asking questions.

Before building the outreach, understand:

### 1. The Valuation/Report
- What did you create? (valuation, benchmark, score, assessment)
- What data points does it include?
- How was it generated? (website scan, public data, industry benchmarks)
- What's the "wow moment" — the single most interesting insight?
- Is there a personalized element (their actual numbers vs. generic)?

### 2. The Target
- What type of business? (agency, SaaS, ecommerce, etc.)
- What's their typical size? (revenue, headcount, etc.)
- Who's the owner/decision maker?
- How did you source them? (manual research, database, scraping)
- Batch size per run?

### 3. The Conversion Goal
- What do you want them to do after seeing the valuation?
  - Book a call
  - Create an account
  - Request a detailed report
  - Start a conversation
- What's the eventual monetization? (advisory, brokerage, SaaS, etc.)

### 4. The Delivery Mechanism
- Where does the valuation live? (web app, PDF, landing page)
- Is there a unique URL per business?
- Do they need to log in or provide info to see it?
- Can you gate parts of it? (free summary → full report behind CTA)

## Core Strategy: The Three-Phase Model

### Phase 1: The Cold Email (Get the Reply)
- **No link in the first email.** Links in cold emails get flagged by spam filters and look salesy.
- Goal: get them to reply with interest.
- Frame: research project, their business was included, results are ready.

### Phase 2: The Delivery (Provide Value)
- Reply with the link to claim their valuation.
- Make the claim experience frictionless.
- The report itself should impress — this is your credibility moment.

### Phase 3: The Conversion (Start the Relationship)
- After they've seen the report, present the next step.
- This is where you introduce the upsell, call booking, or deeper engagement.

---

## Phase 1: Cold Email Strategy

### The Positioning Frame

Pick a frame that makes sense for your project. The frame determines how the entire outreach reads.

**Frame A: The Industry Map** (Recommended for agency valuations)
> "We're mapping the [industry] landscape — analyzing [metric] across [number] companies. Your company was included."

**Frame B: The Benchmark Study**
> "We benchmarked [number] [business type] on [metrics]. Here's where you landed."

**Frame C: The Annual/Quarterly Report**
> "We just published our [Q1 2026 / Annual] [Industry] Report. Your company is featured."

**Frame D: The Index**
> "We're building the [Industry] Index — ranking [business type] by [criteria]. You're on it."

### Email #1: The Opener

**Principles:**
- Short. Under 100 words.
- No link. No attachment. No pitch.
- Specific to their business (use their company name, a detail from their site).
- End with a question that's easy to say yes to.
- Send from a real person's email, not a noreply.

**Structure:**
```
Subject: [Company Name] — [specific detail]
---
[One sentence about what you're doing — the project/research frame.]

[One sentence about their specific inclusion — why them.]

[One sentence about what you found — a teaser, not the full result.]

[Question: Do you want to see the full results?]

[Name]
```

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't say "I hope this email finds you well"
- Don't explain your company or yourself
- Don't include multiple links
- Don't write more than 5-6 sentences
- Don't use HTML-heavy formatting
- Don't use "quick question" or "just checking in" subject lines

### Email #2: The Follow-Up (3-4 days later)

Only send if no reply. Keep it even shorter.

**Principles:**
- Reference the first email briefly
- Add one new piece of information (social proof or a teaser insight)
- Same easy question

### Email #3: The Final Nudge (5-7 days after Email #2)

**Principles:**
- Acknowledge you might be wrong about their interest
- Give them an easy out ("no worries if not")
- Mention a deadline or reason it's time-sensitive (batch expires, report publishes, etc.)

---

## Phase 2: Delivery Strategy

### When They Reply

They said "yes, send it." Now you deliver. This reply matters as much as the first email.

**Principles:**
- Reply quickly (within hours, not days)
- Include the link naturally in a short reply
- Set expectations for what they'll see
- Don't oversell — let the report do the work

**Structure:**
```
Great — here's your report: [link]

[One sentence on what they'll see first.]

[One sentence on what surprised you about their results, if relevant.]

Let me know what you think.
```

### The Claim Page

The link they click should be a dedicated claim/landing page, not a raw dashboard.

**Page structure:**
1. **Headline**: "{Company Name} — Your [Valuation/Benchmark] Report"
2. **Summary snapshot**: 2-3 key numbers visible immediately (no gate)
3. **The full report**: Either visible below or behind a simple action (email, create account)
4. **Context**: How they compare to peers (ranges, not exact competitors)
5. **CTA**: The conversion action — book a call, get detailed analysis, unlock full report

**Key principles:**
- Show them something valuable BEFORE asking for anything
- The first screen should have real data, not a login wall
- Use their company name and details — this is personalized, not generic
- Include methodology notes to build credibility

### What to Gate vs. What to Show Free

**Show free (builds trust):**
- Overall valuation number or score
- 2-3 headline metrics
- Their position in the market (percentile or tier)
- High-level strengths and weaknesses

**Gate behind CTA (drives conversion):**
- Detailed breakdown and methodology
- Specific recommendations
- Comparable transactions or benchmarks
- Downloadable PDF version
- Historical trends or projections

## Phase 3: Conversion Strategy

### After They View the Report

Wait 24-48 hours after they've viewed the report, then follow up.

**Option A: The Insight Follow-Up**
> "Noticed you checked out your report. One thing that stood out — [specific insight about their business]. Happy to walk through what that means if useful."

**Option B: The Peer Comparison**
> "Interesting thing about your results — you're [above/below] average on [metric] compared to similar [business type]. That's usually because of [reason]. Worth a quick chat?"

**Option C: The Direct Ask**
> "Now that you've seen where you stand — want to talk about what it would take to [improve metric / increase valuation / close the gap]?"

### Conversion CTAs (Pick One)

| CTA | Best When |
|-----|-----------|
| Book a 15-min call | High-touch, advisory/brokerage model |
| Create account for updates | SaaS/platform play, long nurture |
| Request detailed report | Lead qualification, gated content |
| Join the benchmark community | Community-led, recurring engagement |
| Get matched with a buyer/partner | Marketplace model |

## Deliverability & Compliance

### Email Deliverability

Cold email deliverability is the #1 risk. Follow these rules:

1. **Warm your domain** — Don't send from a brand new domain. Use a domain that's been active for 30+ days with regular email activity.
2. **Separate sending domain** — Use a subdomain or variant (e.g., `research.yourdomain.com`) to protect your main domain reputation.
3. **No links in Email #1** — This alone dramatically improves inbox placement.
4. **Plain text emails** — No HTML templates, images, or heavy formatting for the cold emails. Plain text looks personal.
5. **Small batches** — 10-20 per day max from a single sending address. Your batch of 10 is fine.
6. **Personalize the first line** — Generic openers trigger spam filters. Mention something specific about their business.
7. **SPF, DKIM, DMARC** — Make sure your sending domain has proper email authentication set up.

### Legal Compliance

- **CAN-SPAM (US)**: Include physical address and unsubscribe option. Business-to-business prospecting emails are permitted but must comply.
- **GDPR (EU/UK)**: Legitimate interest can apply for B2B outreach, but keep records of your basis and honor opt-outs immediately.
- **CASL (Canada)**: More restrictive. Requires implied or express consent. Be careful with Canadian prospects.
- Always honor unsubscribes immediately.
- Include your real identity and business information.

## Personalization Framework

The difference between outreach that works and outreach that gets deleted is personalization. Here's what to personalize for each prospect:

### Must Personalize
- Company name (in subject line and body)
- One specific detail from their website (a service they offer, a case study, a team size indicator)
- Their actual valuation data point (even a teaser — "your agency scored in the top 30%")

### Should Personalize
- Industry or niche reference
- Geographic reference if relevant
- Recent news or change (new hire, award, rebrand)

### How to Scale Personalization at 10 Per Batch

With only 10 per batch, you can (and should) hand-personalize every email.

1. Spend 2-3 minutes per prospect reviewing their website
2. Note one specific thing: a service they highlight, a case study, a team photo, a recent blog post
3. Use that detail in the opening line
4. Reference their actual valuation data

At 10 per batch, this is 20-30 minutes of research. That's the investment that separates you from the spam folder.

## Metrics & Optimization

### What to Track

| Metric | Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Open rate | 60-80% | Subject line + deliverability working |
| Reply rate | 15-30% | Messaging resonance |
| Claim rate | 50-70% of replies | Report delivery + page experience |
| Conversion rate | 20-40% of claims | CTA strength + report quality |
| Unsubscribe/negative reply rate | < 5% | Targeting + tone |

### If Open Rates Are Low (< 40%)
- Test different subject line approaches
- Check deliverability (use mail-tester.com)
- Verify email addresses before sending
- Check if you're landing in spam

### If Reply Rates Are Low (< 10%)
- The email is too long or too salesy
- The frame doesn't feel authentic
- No personalization
- The ask is too big (asking for a call vs. asking "want to see it?")

### If Claim Rates Are Low (< 30% of replies)
- You're taking too long to send the link after they reply
- The claim page is confusing or broken
- They need to do too much to see their results (login wall)

### If Conversion Rates Are Low (< 10% of claims)
- The report itself isn't impressive enough
- The CTA is unclear or too aggressive
- No follow-up after they view the report
- The gap between "free report" and "paid service" is too big

### For Agency Valuations
- `{Agency Name} valuation`
- `{Agency Name} — agency benchmark`
- `{Agency Name} in our Q1 report`
- `Quick question about {Agency Name}`
- `{Agency Name} — {specific metric}`

### For Benchmark Reports
- `How {Company Name} compares`
- `{Company Name} benchmark results`
- `{Industry} benchmark — {Company Name}`

### For Research Framing
- `{Company Name} included in our research`
- `Your agency came up in our analysis`
- `{Company Name} — interesting finding`

**Rules:**
- Always include their company name
- Keep under 50 characters when possible
- No ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation
- No spam trigger words ("free," "guaranteed," "act now")

## Anti-Patterns (What Makes People Ignore You)

You said you get this kind of outreach and ignore it. Here's why, and how to avoid each trap:

| Why People Ignore It | How to Avoid It |
|-----------------------|-----------------|
| Obviously templated | Hand-write for batches of 10. No mail merge feel. |
| "I'd love to help you with..." | You already did the work. Lead with that. |
| Long first email | 4-6 sentences max. Respect their time. |
| Vague value prop | "We valued your agency at $X" > "We help agencies grow" |
| Immediate ask for a call | Ask if they want to see results. Call comes later. |
| HTML-heavy design email | Plain text. Looks like a real person wrote it. |
| Follows up 8 times | Max 3 emails. Know when to stop. |
| No easy out | "No worries if not your thing" shows confidence. |
| Sent to info@ or generic emails | Find the actual owner/founder. |
| Subject line is clickbait | Straightforward + their company name. |

When building a valuation outreach campaign, provide:

### 1. Campaign Overview
```
Project Frame: [The research/benchmark framing]
Target: [Business type, size, niche]
Batch Size: [Number per run]
Valuation Type: [What the report contains]
Conversion Goal: [What you want them to do]
```

### 2. Email Sequence
For each email, provide:
```
Email [#]: [Purpose]
Send: [Timing]
Subject: [Subject line]
Body: [Full copy — plain text]
Goal: [What response you want]
```

### 3. Reply Templates
Pre-written replies for:
- "Yes, send it" → Delivery reply
- "What is this?" → Explanation reply
- "Not interested" → Graceful exit
- "How did you get my info?" → Transparency reply

- Page sections and content
- What's shown free vs. gated
- CTA copy and placement

### 5. Post-Claim Follow-Up
- Timing and trigger
- Message copy
- Conversion CTA
