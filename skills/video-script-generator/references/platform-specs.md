# Platform Specs for Short-Form Video

Reference for technical and algorithmic constraints per platform. Use these when calibrating script length, pacing, and tone.

---

## Platform Comparison

| Platform | Optimal Duration | Aspect Ratio | Words/min | Primary Audience |
|----------|-----------------|--------------|-----------|-----------------|
| TikTok | 21–34s or 54–60s | 9:16 | ~150 | Gen Z, Millennials, broad consumer |
| Instagram Reels | 15–30s (peak), up to 90s | 9:16 | ~150 | Millennials, lifestyle, B2C brands |
| YouTube Shorts | 45–60s | 9:16 | ~140 | Cross-demographic, search-driven |
| LinkedIn Video | 30–90s | 16:9 or 1:1 | ~120 | Professionals, B2B buyers, founders |
| Facebook Video | 60–90s | 16:9 or 1:1 | ~130 | 35–65, local business, communities |

---

## TikTok

**Algorithm behavior:**
- First 2 seconds determine whether TikTok pushes the video
- Completion rate is the #1 ranking signal — scripts that loop well get rewarded
- The algorithm reads captions; front-load the keyword in the first line

**Script implications:**
- Hook must be visual AND spoken simultaneously — don't rely on one channel alone
- Caption text on screen should mirror or complement the spoken word, not repeat it verbatim
- Auto-captions are on by default; write for how words look on screen (avoid long run-on sentences)
- 54–60s sweet spot: long enough to deliver value, short enough to pull high completion

**Pacing:** Faster than you think. Cut every pause. Edit to remove breath gaps.

---

## Instagram Reels

**Algorithm behavior:**
- Shares (sends via DM) are the highest-value engagement signal
- Reels optimized for saves + shares outperform those optimized for likes
- Audio matters more than on any other platform — trending audio gives algorithmic lift
- Seamless loops (end connects to start) boost completion rate and signal quality

**Script implications:**
- Write the last line to naturally flow back into the hook if the video loops
- If using trending audio: write the script around the audio beat drops, not the other way around
- 15–30s is the power zone for reach; 60–90s works for established audiences expecting depth
- On-screen text callouts (key phrases highlighted) increase saves significantly

**Pacing:** Match energy to the visual edit. The script is a track, not a monologue.

---

## YouTube Shorts

**Algorithm behavior:**
- Shorts feed is keyword-driven — the first 3 seconds and the title/description drive discovery
- Unlike TikTok, YouTube weights watch time as a percentage AND absolute duration
- Shorts can drive subscribers to long-form content; CTA strategy matters more here
- Comments are a strong quality signal on Shorts specifically

**Script implications:**
- Open with the keyword or core topic spoken aloud in the first 3 seconds
- Strong CTA toward subscribing or "watch the full video" converts better than purchase CTAs
- 50–60s hits the sweet spot: enough to deliver one complete idea, short enough to replay
- Hook should promise a specific outcome — Shorts viewers are looking for utility

**Pacing:** Steady and clear. YouTube Shorts audience skews toward learning intent; slightly slower delivery works.

---

## LinkedIn Video

**Algorithm behavior:**
- Native video gets 5x more reach than link posts
- First 3–5 seconds autoplay without sound — the visual hook must work silently
- Subtitles/captions are not auto-generated: always burn them in or add them in the post
- LinkedIn's algorithm rewards dwell time and comments over likes

**Script implications:**
- Assume 60–80% of viewers will watch without sound; captions are not optional
- Tone must be professional but not sterile — a point of view and mild controversy drive comments
- Open with a bold statement or counterintuitive claim (works without sound as on-screen text)
- Script should feel like a sharp conference talk, not a marketing ad
- Avoid direct hard sell; end with a question or insight that invites comment

**Business model fit:** B2B only. Service businesses targeting enterprise or mid-market.

**Pacing:** Deliberate, measured. Leave slight pauses after key points — LinkedIn viewers watch on desktop with audio.

---

## Facebook Video

**Algorithm behavior:**
- Watch time past 3 seconds separates "viewed" from "scrolled past" in Facebook's metrics
- Videos in Facebook Groups get 2–3x more organic reach than page posts
- Captioned videos get 12% more watch time on average (Facebook's own data)
- Shares in communities are the fastest reach multiplier

**Script implications:**
- Audience skews older (35–65); avoid slang and internet-native references
- Community angle works well: speak to a shared identity ("If you run a service business in [city]...")
- Longer scripts (60–90s) work for trust-building content — this audience is more patient
- End with a question that invites commenting, not just clicking

**Business model fit:** Service businesses, local businesses, community-driven B2C brands.

---

## Silent-First Principle

All platforms autoplay without sound in some contexts (feed scroll, muted device). Design every script with a visual layer:

- The first on-screen text should convey the hook even with no audio
- Key claims should appear as text overlays, not just be spoken
- Facial expression and body language must carry urgency when voice can't

Write the visual notes in your script. A script without visual direction is half a script.
