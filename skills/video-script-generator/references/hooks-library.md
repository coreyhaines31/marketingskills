# Hooks Library

Opening lines that stop the scroll. Organized by psychological trigger. Each hook is a pattern — swap in your specific details.

The rule: **the first sentence is the only sentence that matters until the viewer decides to keep watching.** Everything else is secondary.

---

## Curiosity Hooks

Open a loop the viewer needs to close. Make them feel like they're missing something.

- "The [X]% of [audience] who get this right are doing something everyone else ignores."
- "There's a reason [common behavior] never works. And it's not what you think."
- "Nobody talks about [specific aspect of your topic]. Here's why that's a problem."
- "I asked [impressive number] [audience] what their biggest mistake was. The answer surprised me."
- "Before you [common next step], you need to know this."
- "The thing holding most [audience] back isn't [what they assume]. It's this."

---

## Controversy / Contrarian Hooks

Challenge a belief your audience holds. Create productive friction.

- "Stop [popular tactic]. It's not helping you — here's proof."
- "[Industry-standard advice] is outdated. This is what actually works in [current year]."
- "I know this is going to be controversial, but: [bold direct claim]."
- "The [common tool / method] everyone recommends is costing you [specific thing]."
- "Hot take: [belief your audience has] is exactly why [negative outcome] keeps happening."
- "[Respected figure or approach] had it backwards. Here's what the data actually shows."

---

## Number / List Hooks

Concrete, scannable, makes a specific promise. Works best for educational content.

- "[Number] things successful [audience] do before [time / milestone] that nobody posts about."
- "I made [number of] mistakes in [domain] before I learned [specific lesson]. Here they are."
- "[Number] signs your [process/tool/strategy] is already broken."
- "Only [small number]% of [audience] know this. The rest are leaving [result] on the table."
- "[Number] questions to ask yourself before you [common decision]."

---

## Direct Promise Hooks

Tell them exactly what they'll walk away with. No mystery — pure utility signal.

- "In the next [60 seconds / 2 minutes], I'll show you how to [specific desirable outcome]."
- "If you do [specific action] right now, [specific result] happens. Let me show you."
- "Here's the exact [process / script / framework] I use to [outcome]. No fluff."
- "This is the [tool / method] I'd use if I was starting over with [zero resource constraint]."
- "By the end of this, you'll know exactly what to do about [their problem]."

---

## Story / Personal Hooks

Start mid-story. Drop the viewer into a real moment. No setup — start at the tension.

- "Last [week/month/year], I almost [significant failure or loss]. Here's what actually happened."
- "The day I [pivotal moment] was the day everything changed for my [business/career/life]."
- "I lost [specific thing — client, money, opportunity]. Best thing that ever happened to me. Here's why."
- "Three years ago I was [humble/struggling starting point]. Today, [specific result]. This is what shifted."
- "A [client/friend/stranger] sent me a message that stopped me cold. It said: [quote]."
- "I've never told this story publicly. But if it helps even one person, it's worth sharing."

---

## Belief-Challenging Hooks

Attack the assumption that's keeping them stuck. Make them question something they take for granted.

- "You've been told [common belief]. That's not wrong — but it's incomplete. Here's what's missing."
- "What if [the thing they think is the problem] is actually the symptom, not the cause?"
- "Most [audience] are optimizing for the wrong thing. Here's what actually moves the needle."
- "The reason [desired outcome] feels so hard isn't effort — it's [reframe of the actual issue]."
- "[Widely held belief] is technically true. But acting on it is exactly why [negative outcome] happens."

---

## Urgency / Context Hooks

Tie the content to a specific moment, deadline, or market condition. Creates FOMO without being manipulative.

- "If you're [in this situation] right now, this is exactly what you need to hear."
- "Something changed in [platform/industry/market] in [timeframe]. Most people haven't noticed yet."
- "This is working right now. In six months, everyone will know it — and it'll stop working."
- "The window for [opportunity] is closing. Here's how to act before it does."
- "I wouldn't be sharing this if it wasn't [directly relevant to their current situation]."

---

## Data / Surprising Stat Hooks

Lead with a number that creates cognitive dissonance. Forces a "wait, really?" reaction.

- "[Counterintuitive statistic]. Let that sink in."
- "The average [audience] spends [surprisingly large amount] on [thing] — and gets [poor result]. Here's the fix."
- "[X]% of [audience] fail at [common goal] within [timeframe]. This is the one reason why."
- "I analyzed [impressive data set]. The pattern that showed up was not what I expected."
- "According to [credible source], [surprising finding]. Here's what that means for [audience]."

---

## Silent Hook Openers (Text-on-Screen for Autoplay)

When designing for silent autoplay (LinkedIn, Facebook, mobile feed), the on-screen text IS the hook. These work even with no audio:

- **Bold single word, full screen:** "WRONG." / "STOP." / "WAIT."
- **Short provocative question:** "Why does no one talk about this?"
- **Counter + claim:** "3 years. 1 mistake. Here's what I should have done."
- **Direct address:** "If you [do this], keep watching."
- **Teaser for what's coming:** "The [result] came from this one change →"

## Strategic Hesitation Phrases

Drop these naturally into scripts to signal authenticity. They work because AI never pauses to admit uncertainty or take a real stance.

**Opening hesitations (hook amplifiers):**
- "I'll be honest with you..."
- "Look, I'm not going to sugarcoat this..."
- "This is going to sound obvious, but stay with me..."
- "I debated whether to share this, but..."

**Mid-script hesitations (credibility builders):**
- "And here's the part that most people skip..."
- "I wish someone had told me this sooner..."
- "This next part is where most people check out. Don't."
- "No, seriously — write this down."

**Closing hesitations (CTA softeners):**
- "If nothing else, take this with you..."
- "I could be wrong about this, but the results don't lie..."
- "Take it or leave it — but this is what works for me."

## Anti-AI Term Reference (Full List)

Words and phrases that signal machine-generated content to trained audiences. Audit every script against this list before finalizing.

| Never use | Why it flags | Alternative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| "In today's landscape" | Generic, dateless | "Right now" / "Today in [year]" |
| "Leverage" (as verb) | Corporate emptiness | "Use" / "Put to work" / "Make the most of" |
| "Crucial" / "Pivotal" | Inflated without evidence | "Important" / "Key" / "Worth noting" |
| "Transform" / "Revolutionize" | Hyperbole without proof | "Change" / "Improve" / "Shift" |
| "Delve into" | Academic, AI tell | "Get into" / "Look at" / "Break down" |
| "Comprehensive" | Vague promise | Be specific about what you're covering |
| "Robust" | Means nothing | Describe the actual capability |
| "Seamless" | Overused filler | Describe what it actually does smoothly |
| "Game-changer" | Highest-effort lowest-signal phrase | Describe the actual change |
| "It's worth noting that" | Unnecessary preface | Just say the thing |
| "In conclusion" | Signals you ran out of ideas | Just end |
| "Navigate" (challenges/landscape) | Jargon | "Handle" / "Deal with" / "Work through" |
| "Empower" | Corporate, passive | "Let you" / "Help you" / "Give you the ability to" |
| "Foster" | Nonprofit-speak | "Build" / "Create" / "Grow" |
| "Synergy" | Dead on arrival | Describe the actual collaborative benefit |
| "Moving forward" | Filler transition | Cut it entirely |
| "At the end of the day" | Cliché closer | Just say the conclusion |
| "Think outside the box" | Self-defeating cliché | Be specific about what unconventional thing you mean |
