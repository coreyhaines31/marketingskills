# Script Templates

Five ready-to-use structures with scripted examples. Adapt the template to your niche, audience, and platform. Fill in the [brackets].

---

## Template 1: Educational ("Did You Know")

**Best for:** B2B, authority-building, subject-matter expertise
**Framework:** Hook-Retain-Reward
**Optimal length:** 45–90 seconds

**Structure:**
1. Hook — counterintuitive stat or claim that challenges an assumption
2. Familiar problem — name the pain they already feel
3. Root cause — the insight most people miss
4. Solution — specific and actionable (not vague)
5. CTA — low friction, tied to the solution

**Template:**

```
[0–3s] HOOK
Script: "[Surprising statistic or claim about your industry]. Most [audience] don't know this."
Visual: [Speaker to camera, confident, direct eye contact. Stat appears as text overlay.]

[3–12s] PROBLEM
Script: "Here's what's actually happening. You're [common behavior], and it's costing you [specific
         consequence]. Not because you're doing it wrong — because nobody told you the real reason
         it's not working."
Visual: [Cut to screen recording, whiteboard, or relevant b-roll showing the problem in action.]

[12–45s] INSIGHT + SOLUTION
Script: "The real issue is [root cause]. When you fix that one thing — specifically [action] —
         everything else clicks into place. Here's exactly how: [step 1], [step 2], [step 3]."
Visual: [Step callouts on screen. Keep it visual — don't just talk. Show the thing.]

[Final 5s] CTA
Script: "If this helped, follow for [specific content they'll get]. I post [cadence]."
Visual: [Speaker back to camera. Simple, direct close — no fade, no music swell.]
```

**Word count target:** 130–180 words for 60–90s

## Template 2: Storytelling (Before / After)

**Best for:** Service businesses, personal brand, coaches, consultants
**Framework:** Hook-Retain-Reward
**Optimal length:** 45–75 seconds

**Structure:**
1. Hook — the after state (start at the end)
2. Before — where you/client started (make it relatable)
3. Breaking point — the moment everything changed
4. The discovery — what caused the shift
5. Result — specific and credible
6. CTA — invitation to the same result

**Template:**

```
[0–3s] HOOK
Script: "[Specific impressive result]. [Timeframe]. And [context that makes it believable —
         e.g., 'starting from zero' or 'after years of doing it the wrong way']."
Visual: [Show the result first — the finished product, the number, the transformation. Let it speak.]

[3–10s] BEFORE
Script: "Twelve months ago, I was [relatable struggling state]. [One specific detail that makes
         it real — a number, a situation, a feeling they'll recognize]."
Visual: [Cut to "before" visual — old work, old environment, or speaker being direct to camera.]

[10–40s] TURNING POINT + DISCOVERY
Script: "Then [specific moment of change]. I stopped [old behavior] and started [new behavior].
         The difference? [Core insight in one sentence]. That's it. That's the whole shift."
Visual: [Visualize the turning point — even a simple diagram or gesture works. Don't just talk.]

[40–55s] RESULT
Script: "Since then: [Result 1]. [Result 2]. [Result 3]. These aren't hypotheticals.
         [Grounding detail that adds credibility — client name, timeframe, screenshot]."
Visual: [Show the proof — metrics, testimonial screenshot, before/after visual.]

[Final 5s] CTA
Script: "If you want [specific outcome], [specific low-friction action]."
Visual: [Direct to camera. No fluff. End on the action.]
```

## Template 3: Product Demo

**Best for:** E-commerce, SaaS, physical products
**Framework:** Stair-stepping
**Optimal length:** 20–45 seconds

**Structure:**
1. Hook — the problem happening in real life (show, don't tell)
2. Friction — the moment the old solution fails
3. Demo — show the product solving it, fast
4. Result — the outcome, specific and visible
5. CTA — purchase or trial, direct

**Template:**

```
[0–3s] HOOK
Script: "You know that moment when [frustrating situation that the product solves]?"
Visual: [Show the problem happening — the spill, the broken thing, the slow process. Real, not staged.]

[3–8s] FRICTION
Script: "And then you try [common alternative] and it just... [fails in the specific way it fails]."
Visual: [Show the old solution failing. Make it slightly comedic if tone allows.]

[8–25s] DEMO
Script: "This is [product]. [One-sentence value prop — what it does and for whom]. Watch."
Visual: [Product in action. No cuts, no fancy transitions — real-time if possible.
         Show the hands, show the product, show it working. Speed ramp to the good part.]

[25–35s] RESULT
Script: "[Specific measurable outcome]. [Time it took]. [One line that makes it feel real —
         a detail, a reaction, a comparison]."
Visual: [The finished result — clean, satisfying close-up. Let it breathe for 1–2 seconds.]

[Final 5s] CTA
Script: "Link in bio. [Any urgency trigger — limited run, discount code, shipping deadline]."
Visual: [Product hero shot with CTA text on screen.]
```

## Template 4: Authority / Point of View (B2B)

**Best for:** LinkedIn, founder brand, B2B thought leadership
**Framework:** Hook-Retain-Reward
**Optimal length:** 45–90 seconds

**Structure:**
1. Hook — a contrarian or surprising observation from your experience
2. The common mistake — what most people in this space do wrong
3. Your take — your specific perspective, backed by real experience
4. The implication — what changes when you see it this way
5. CTA — low-friction (follow, comment, DM)

**Template:**

```
[0–4s] HOOK
Script: "After [X years / X clients / X in revenue], here's the thing I can't stop seeing:
         [contrarian observation that challenges conventional wisdom in your industry]."
Visual: [Speaker direct to camera, confident. Minimal background clutter. Captions on.]

[4–15s] THE PROBLEM MOST PEOPLE HAVE
Script: "Most [role/audience] think [common belief]. And I get it — that's what everyone says.
         But that framing is exactly why [negative outcome they're experiencing] keeps happening."
Visual: [Stay on speaker. No need for b-roll here — the argument IS the content.]

[15–50s] YOUR POV
Script: "Here's how I actually see it: [your framework, observation, or reframe in plain language].
         I've seen this with [anonymized real example — not hypothetical]. When [behavior A],
         the result is [outcome A]. Switch to [behavior B], and [outcome B] happens. Every time."
Visual: [Optional: cut to a simple diagram, whiteboard, or text callout for the key distinction.
         Return to speaker for the close.]

[Final 10s] IMPLICATION + CTA
Script: "The uncomfortable truth: [one sharp takeaway]. If that landed, follow — I post [frequency]
         on [topic]. Or tell me below: [question that invites a comment]."
Visual: [Speaker direct to camera. End with the question on screen as a text overlay.]
```

## Template 5: High-Retention Hook (MrBeast-style)

**Best for:** Awareness, entertainment, viral reach on TikTok/Reels/Shorts
**Framework:** Stair-stepping
**Optimal length:** 30–60 seconds

**Structure:**
1. Visual promise — show the most impressive thing that will happen
2. Stakes — why anyone should care
3. Escalation 1 — first level of progress or intensity
4. Escalation 2 — raise the stakes further
5. Abrupt payoff — deliver the climax exactly when promised, then cut

**Template:**

```
[0–3s] HOOK — VISUAL PROMISE
Script: "I'm going to [dramatic action / challenge / result] in [specific timeframe]."
Visual: [Flash the end result — the goal, the prize, the finished thing. Don't hide it.
         This is the thumbnail promise made real in the first 3 seconds.]

[3–10s] STAKES
Script: "If I [fail condition], then [consequence]. No edits. No second chances."
Visual: [Establish the environment, the rules, the stakes. Quick cuts. Energy up.]

[10–25s] ESCALATION 1
Script: "[Narrate what's happening as it happens. Short sentences. React to what you see.]"
Visual: [Show the work, the challenge, the obstacle. Cut frequently — every 2–4 seconds.
         Add text callouts for key moments. Speed ramp dramatic parts.]

[25–45s] ESCALATION 2
Script: "[Raise the stakes — a complication appears, a countdown starts, the goal gets harder.]
         I wasn't expecting [complication]. This changes everything."
Visual: [Tighter cuts. Increase pacing. On-screen timer or counter if applicable.]

[45–58s] PAYOFF
Script: "[Deliver the result — minimal words. Let the visual do the work.]"
Visual: [The climax — wide shot, then close-up. Reaction shot if person is on camera.
         One beat of silence or a peak audio moment.]

[58–60s] CUT
Script: [No outro. No "thanks for watching." End on the payoff.]
Visual: [Hard cut to black or brand frame. Do not wind down.]
```

**Note on the hard cut:** Abrupt endings signal confidence. They also drive replays — viewers want to see the ending again to process it. Engineered completion rate.

## Quick Adaptation Guide

| If your product/service is... | Use template | Modify this |
|-------------------------------|--------------|-------------|
| A complex B2B tool | 4 (Authority) | Add ROI numbers to the "result" |
| A physical consumer product | 3 (Demo) | Make the "friction" relatable and slightly funny |
| A coaching / consulting offer | 2 (Before/After) | Use a client story, not your own, for credibility |
| An educational resource | 1 (Educational) | Front-load the stat — make the hook the most surprising thing |
| An event or challenge | 5 (High-retention) | Use real-time filming; avoid heavy scripting |
