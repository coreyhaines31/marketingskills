# Anti-Patterns: AI-Generated Web Design Fingerprints

This reference catalogs every common pattern that makes AI-generated websites instantly recognizable. Each entry includes the pattern, why it's a fingerprint, and what to do instead.

---

## Category 1: The Tailwind Defaults

AI coding agents learn from millions of Tailwind examples and converge on the same utility combinations.

### 1.1 The Universal Card

**Fingerprint:**
```html
<div class="rounded-lg shadow-md p-6 bg-white">
  <Icon class="w-8 h-8 text-blue-500 mb-4" />
  <h3 class="text-lg font-semibold mb-2">Feature Title</h3>
  <p class="text-gray-600 text-sm">Description of the feature goes here.</p>
</div>
```

**Why it's a tell:** Every AI generates this exact pattern — rounded card, small icon, `text-lg` heading, `text-gray-600` body. Seen on 90%+ of AI-built sites.

**Device replacement:** Full-width strip with left border accent, large type, no icon.

### 1.2 The Gradient Hero

**Fingerprint:**
```html
<div class="bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-600 to-purple-600 text-white py-20">
  <h1 class="text-4xl font-bold">Transform Your Workflow</h1>
  <p class="text-xl text-white/80 mt-4">The all-in-one platform...</p>
  <button class="bg-white text-blue-600 px-6 py-3 rounded-lg mt-8">Get Started</button>
</div>
```

**Why it's a tell:** Blue-to-purple gradient is the #1 AI design fingerprint. The exact structure (H1 + subtitle + white button on gradient) appears in nearly every AI-generated landing page.

**Device replacement:** Solid dark background, massive display type (7xl+), no gradient, oversized action button.

### 1.3 The Safe Button

**Fingerprint:**
```html
<button class="bg-blue-600 text-white px-4 py-2 rounded-lg text-sm font-medium hover:bg-blue-700">
  Get Started
</button>
```

**Why it's a tell:** Small padding, small text, blue-600, rounded-lg, standard hover darkening. This button exists on every AI-generated site.

**Device replacement:** Full-width, 80px+ height, monospace uppercase, sharp corners, color-invert hover.

### 1.4 The Cautious Spacing

**Fingerprint:** `px-4 py-2`, `p-6`, `space-y-4`, `gap-4` — the same 16px increments everywhere.

**Why it's a tell:** Creates a uniform "safe" rhythm. Real designers use intentionally uneven spacing.

**Device replacement:** Dramatic asymmetry — combine `p-0` with `p-16`. Use `gap-px` (1px gaps) or `gap-16` (64px gaps), nothing in between.

## Category 2: The Template Structure

### 2.1 The Five-Act Website

**Fingerprint:**
```
Act 1: Navbar (logo left, links center, CTA right)
Act 2: Hero (big text, subtitle, button, maybe illustration)
Act 3: Features (3 or 4 cards in a grid)
Act 4: Social Proof (testimonial slider or logos)
Act 5: Footer CTA + Big Footer
```

**Why it's a tell:** This exact structure appears on every AI-generated marketing site. It's the "standard" that makes everything look standard.

**Device replacement:** No acts. The entire page is ONE interface. Single viewport, single purpose. If you scroll, each "section" is a full viewport with one element.

### 2.2 The Navbar

**Fingerprint:**
```html
<nav class="flex items-center justify-between px-6 py-4 bg-white shadow-sm">
  <Logo />
  <div class="flex gap-6">
    <a>Features</a> <a>Pricing</a> <a>About</a> <a>Blog</a>
  </div>
  <button class="bg-blue-600 text-white px-4 py-2 rounded-lg">Sign Up</button>
</nav>
```

**Why it's a tell:** This exact layout is generated by every AI for every project. It signals "template" immediately.

**Device replacement:** Bottom bar with 2-3 text labels, OR floating single menu button (top-right), OR no nav at all (single-page device).

### 2.3 The Three-Column Feature Grid

**Fingerprint:**
```html
<div class="grid grid-cols-3 gap-8">
  <Card icon="⚡" title="Fast" desc="..." />
  <Card icon="🔒" title="Secure" desc="..." />
  <Card icon="🚀" title="Scalable" desc="..." />
</div>
```

**Why it's a tell:** Three cards, each with emoji/icon + adjective title + vague description. The holy trinity of AI web design.

**Device replacement:** Full-width stacked strips (each feature as a horizontal bar), or a 2x2 grid of large clickable blocks with single-word labels.

## Category 3: Color and Typography Tells

### 3.1 The AI Color Stack

**Fingerprint colors (used together):**
- Primary: `blue-600` or `indigo-600`
- Background: `white` or `gray-50`
- Text: `gray-900` for headings, `gray-600` for body
- Accent: `purple-500` or `violet-500`
- Success: `green-500`

**Why it's a tell:** This is literally the default Tailwind palette used without customization.

**Device replacement:** Monochrome base + single saturated accent. Never use blue-600 or indigo-600 as primary.

### 3.2 The Safe Font Stack

**Fingerprint:**
```css
font-family: 'Inter', system-ui, sans-serif;
/* or */ font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Arial, sans-serif;
/* or */ font-family: ui-sans-serif, system-ui, sans-serif;
```

**Why it's a tell:** AI always reaches for Inter, system-ui, or other "safe" choices. These fonts are invisible — they say nothing about the brand.

**Device replacement:** Fonts with extreme personality: Space Mono, Clash Display, Syne, Fraunces, Cabinet Grotesk.

### 3.3 The Timid Type Scale

**Fingerprint:**
```
h1: text-3xl or text-4xl (30-36px)
h2: text-2xl (24px)
h3: text-lg (18px)
body: text-base or text-sm (14-16px)
```

**Why it's a tell:** This scale is "correct" by conventional standards but produces sites that all look the same. No visual drama.

**Device replacement:** Extreme scale: headlines at 72-128px, body at 24-36px. Skip the middle entirely.

## Category 4: Interaction Tells

### 4.1 The Smooth Hover

**Fingerprint:**
```css
transition: all 0.3s ease-in-out;
/* hover: slightly darker, slight scale, maybe shadow */
```

**Why it's a tell:** Smooth, gradual, predictable. Every AI-generated hover behaves this way.

**Device replacement:** Instant state change (`duration-0` or `duration-50`), full color inversion on hover, `scale-95` on press (tactile snap).

### 4.2 The Fade-In Scroll Animation

**Fingerprint:** Elements fade-in and slide-up as user scrolls (AOS, Framer Motion defaults).

**Why it's a tell:** Used on every "modern" AI-generated site. Creates the same floaty feeling.

**Device replacement:** No scroll animations. Elements are present immediately. If animation is needed, use mechanical `steps()` timing.

### 4.3 The Standard Cursor

**Fingerprint:** Default cursor everywhere, pointer on buttons.

**Device replacement:** `cursor-crosshair` on interactive areas for a device/tool feel.

## Category 5: Content Tells

### 5.1 The Buzzword Headline

**Fingerprint examples:**
- "Transform Your Workflow with AI-Powered Solutions"
- "The All-in-One Platform for Modern Teams"
- "Streamline, Optimize, and Scale Your Business"
- "Built for the Future of [Industry]"

**Why it's a tell:** These headlines communicate nothing specific. Every AI generates them.

**Device replacement:** 1-4 words maximum. Concrete noun or verb. "DEPLOY." "Track everything." "One inbox."

### 5.2 The Paragraph Subtitle

**Fingerprint:** A 2-3 sentence paragraph under the headline explaining what the product does.

**Why it's a tell:** Real products with strong positioning don't need explanation paragraphs.

**Device replacement:** One line maximum. Or eliminate the subtitle entirely — let the product name and action button speak.

### 5.3 The Verbose CTA

**Fingerprint:** "Get Started Today", "Start Your Free Trial Now", "Schedule a Demo"

**Device replacement:** Single verb or short command: "START", "OPEN", "TRY IT", "ENTER"

## Category 6: Structural Tells

### 6.1 The Max-Width Container

**Fingerprint:** `max-w-7xl mx-auto px-4` wrapping every section.

**Why it's a tell:** Creates identical page width on every AI site, with identical centered content.

**Device replacement:** Full-bleed sections, OR `max-w-lg` narrow center column, OR asymmetric layout with content pushed to one side.

### 6.2 The Symmetrical Footer

**Fingerprint:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Logo   Links1   Links2   Links3    │
│        Link     Link     Link      │
│        Link     Link     Link      │
│                                    │
│ © 2024 Company. All rights reserved│
└────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Why it's a tell:** Every AI generates this 3-4 column footer with link lists.

**Device replacement:** Single line: `© 2024 · Terms · Privacy` — or no footer at all. The page IS the product.

### 6.3 The Testimonial Section

**Fingerprint:**
```html
<div class="bg-gray-50 py-16">
  <div class="grid grid-cols-3 gap-8">
    <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow-sm">
      <p class="text-gray-600 italic">"This product changed our lives..."</p>
      <div class="flex items-center mt-4">
        <img class="w-10 h-10 rounded-full" />
        <div class="ml-3">
          <p class="font-semibold">Name</p>
          <p class="text-sm text-gray-500">Title, Company</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
```

**Device replacement:** Single powerful metric instead: `12,847 teams` in massive type. Or one quote, full-screen, huge type, attributed in monospace below.

## The 10-Second Test

After applying the device design system, screenshot the page and apply this test:

1. Show it to someone who has never seen the product
2. Ask: "What built this?"
3. If they say "a website builder" or "AI" or "a template" — you failed
4. If they say "I don't know" or "a design agency" or "is this an app?" — you succeeded

The goal is not to look expensive. The goal is to look **intentional**.
