# Confirmation Page Best Practices

## What Is a Confirmation Page & Why It Matters

A **confirmation page** is the first page prospects land on after booking a call, registering for a webinar, or completing any opt-in action. Most businesses waste this page with a generic "Thanks, see you soon!" — a costly mistake.

Done right, a confirmation page:
- **Acknowledges** the action (confirms they're booked)
- **Addresses objections** before prospects have time to bail
- **Guides modern due diligence** — they will Google you; control what they find
- **Warms them up** so they arrive at the call 80% sold
- **Increases show rates** from 50–60% to 80%+

---

## Modern Due Diligence: The Core Concept

After booking, prospects immediately research you. They search:
- "[Your Name] + scam" or "[Company] + reviews"
- Reddit threads, Trustpilot, BBB, YouTube rants
- Price justification, risk, guarantees, timelines

**If you don't guide this research, they'll fill the gaps with fear.**

Your confirmation page must proactively address every category of concern:

| Concern Category | What They're Actually Asking |
|---|---|
| Legitimacy & Credibility | "Is this real? Have they scammed anyone?" |
| Price Objections | "Why is this expensive? Can I get it cheaper?" |
| Risk / Worst-Case | "What if it fails? Is the guarantee real?" |
| Timeline | "How long until I see results? What's required of me?" |
| Success Expectations | "Are testimonials cherry-picked? What's realistic?" |
| Social Proof | "What do Reddit/Trustpilot users say?" |

## Page Structure: The Proven Framework

### 1. Main Confirmation Video (Top of Page)

A short primary video (2–5 min) that does four things:
1. **Welcomes & thanks** them (briefly)
2. **Explains** you'll guide their research — you're on their side
3. **Addresses major risks and objections** — don't shy away from worst-case scenarios
4. **Redirects** them to breakout videos and testimonials below

**Tone:** Transparent, direct, confident. No hype, no fluff. Say plainly: *"I'm here to help you do your research so that by the time we speak, you know exactly whether this is right for you."*

**Own the negative:** If negative threads exist online, mention them and frame them. This disarms skepticism faster than anything.

---

### 2. Breakout Videos (Core of the Page)

A grid/cluster of **short videos (1–5 min each)**, each tackling a specific objection or question. Place immediately below the main video.

**How to select topics:**
- Talk to your sales closers — what objections eat up call time or kill deals?
- Check Reddit/Google for what people say about you
- Look at reviews (positive and negative) for recurring themes

**Example breakout video titles:**
- "How Much Does This Cost?" / "Why It Costs $X"
- "What If It Doesn't Work For Me?"
- "Our Real Success Rate (Including Less-Than-Ideal Outcomes)"
- "How Does It Work / What Do You Get?"
- "What's Our Guarantee — And What If You're Unhappy?"
- "How Long Until I See Results?"
- "How Much Time/Capital Do I Need?"
- "What Do Real Customers Say on Reddit/Trustpilot?"

**Crafting each breakout video:**
1. **Title clearly** — name the exact objection
2. **Open with the question** — "A lot of people ask us..."
3. **Answer with evidence** — dispute rate screenshots, refund policy, customer stories
4. **Invite further review** — "Bring any remaining questions to the call"
5. **Keep it under 5 minutes** — micro-attention spans are real

**Priority order:** Put the #1 objection first (usually price or "is this legit?")

---

### 3. Testimonials & Trust Assets

Modern prospects want the **full picture**, not just highlight reels.

**Three tiers of testimonials to include:**
1. **Home-run wins** — the big results (wow factor, aspirational)
2. **Average/mid-level wins** — realistic for most buyers, increases credibility
3. **Worst-case or partial wins** — someone who barely broke even but still found value. This *builds immense trust* because it signals you're not hiding failures.

**Trust asset types:**
- Video testimonials (most powerful)
- Third-party platform links (Trustpilot, Reddit, BBB — link to real reviews)
- Data points: "0.7% dispute rate after 5,000 transactions"
- Known endorsements or brand logos (as seen on...)
- Dispute/refund rate screenshots if favorable

**Platform strategy:**
- Point prospects to Reddit threads about you
- Direct them to Trustpilot with context: *"Trustpilot is a credible platform where businesses cannot delete reviews — these are unfiltered"*
- Embed or link authentic video interviews with clients who walk through their journey

---

### 4. Pre-Call Checklist

A simple set of instructions that prepares them for the call:
- **Calendar:** Check email for calendar invite; add to calendar
- **Punctuality:** Be ready on time; emphasize you respect their time and expect the same
- **Environment:** Be in a quiet spot, not on the go
- **Mindset:** Come with questions; be ready to make a decision if it's a fit
- **Optional:** Join community (Telegram, Facebook group) to see live results

## Follow-Up Sequence (Email, Text, Retargeting)

The confirmation page is the anchor; the follow-up sequence amplifies it.

**Email sequence:**
- Go beyond "Don't forget your call"
- Link back to specific breakout videos they may have missed
- Highlight trust assets: case studies, average results, a big win, a partial win

**Retargeting (Facebook, Instagram, YouTube):**
- Show short-form content reinforcing confirmation page points
- If call is 2–3 days out, they should see multiple pieces from you addressing objections

**Text/Call reminders:**
- 24-hour reminder
- 2-hour reminder
- 10-minute reminder
- Include a link back to the confirmation page for anyone who hasn't watched the content

## Show Rate Benchmarks & Optimization

| Show Rate | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 80%+ | Excellent | Maintain and refresh quarterly |
| 70–79% | Good | Review highest-exit breakout topics |
| Below 70% | Red flag | Overhaul trust assets and breakout content |

**Why show rates improve:**
- Objections are pre-answered before the call
- Prospects feel informed and respected (not sold to)
- Trust is established before the phone rings

**What to watch:**
- Ask closers which objections are still killing deals → make new breakout videos
- If no-shows mention a specific concern, add a video addressing it
- Refresh content quarterly — new rumors, new objections emerge

## Sales Cycle Impact

When you execute this correctly:
- **More one-call closes** — prospects arrive already knowing price, risks, and guarantees
- **More "paid in full" commitments** — fewer installment requests
- **Shorter calls** — less time spent proving legitimacy
- **Better qualified leads** — unqualified prospects self-select out before the call

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- ❌ Generic "Thanks for booking!" with no content
- ❌ Only showing best-case testimonials
- ❌ Ignoring what's being said about you on Reddit/Google
- ❌ No breakout videos — making prospects ask the same questions on every call
- ❌ Set-and-forget — never updating the page as objections evolve
- ❌ Reminders that don't link back to the confirmation page content

## Quick-Start Checklist

When building or auditing a confirmation page:

- [ ] Main welcome/research guidance video (top of page)
- [ ] Price objection breakout video
- [ ] "What if it doesn't work?" breakout video
- [ ] Success rate / realistic expectations breakout video
- [ ] Process/how-it-works breakout video
- [ ] Guarantee / risk breakout video
- [ ] Testimonials: big wins, average wins, partial wins
- [ ] Third-party review links (Trustpilot, Reddit) with context
- [ ] Dispute rate or other quantifiable trust data
- [ ] Pre-call checklist (calendar, punctuality, environment)
- [ ] Email/text follow-up sequence linking back to page
- [ ] Retargeting ads running to booked prospects
- [ ] Show rate tracking set up
- [ ] Quarterly review scheduled
