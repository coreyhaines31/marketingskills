# NÉVUE Brand Kit

> **For AI agents:** Read this file before creating any copy, design briefs, ad creative, social content, email templates, or sales collateral. Every output must be consistent with these guidelines. Reference `brand-tokens.json` for exact color values and `fonts.md` for typography rules.

---

## Brand Overview

**Brand name:** NÉVUE  
**Pronunciation:** Neh-vyoo  
**Accent mark:** Always include the accent on É — never write "NEVUE"  
**Capitalization:** All caps in display contexts (NÉVUE); title case in body text (Névue)

**Brand essence:** Luxury wellness elevated to the level of fine art. NÉVUE is a premium medical aesthetics and IV wellness brand that merges clinical efficacy with a five-star hospitality experience. Think: the precision of medicine, the warmth of a spa, the elegance of a French luxury house.

**Positioning:** NÉVUE is not a medspa. It is a wellness atelier.

## Brand Personality

### The Five Pillars
1. **Elevated** — Everything feels considered, intentional, and refined. Nothing is casual.
2. **Clinical Confidence** — Backed by science, delivered with expertise. Not trendy; timeless.
3. **Warm Authority** — We are not cold or sterile. We are knowledgeable and welcoming.
4. **Discreet Luxury** — Quiet confidence over loud status. The brand whispers, never shouts.
5. **Transformative** — Every interaction should leave someone feeling — and looking — better.

### We are:
- Sophisticated, poised, precise
- Warm but never casual
- Premium but never pretentious
- Confident but never arrogant
- Modern but rooted in timeless elegance

### We are not:
- Trendy, buzzy, or hype-driven
- Clinical or cold
- Discount-oriented or promotional
- Loud or attention-seeking
- Generic "spa" aesthetic

## Color Palette

*See `brand-tokens.json` for exact HEX, RGB, HSL, and CMYK values.*

### Primary Colors

#### Névue Navy — `#1B2744`
The dominant brand color. Deep, rich, authoritative navy with a slight blue-purple depth. Used in the building facade, packaging, candle vessel, gift boxes, and staff uniforms. This is the brand's backbone — it communicates authority, trust, and luxury simultaneously.

- **Use for:** Primary backgrounds, wordmark on light surfaces, primary buttons, packaging, apparel
- **Never use:** As a text color on dark backgrounds; on top of Névue Gold (use sparingly)
- **Emotion:** Trust, authority, depth, sophistication

#### Névue Gold — `#C9A96E`
A warm champagne gold — not yellow, not bronze. The exact tone of a fine champagne glass, brushed gold hardware, or embossed foil. Used for the logo embroidery, candle label, door hardware, and accent elements.

- **Use for:** Logo accent color, embossed elements, CTA borders, accent lines, icons, small highlight text on navy
- **Never use:** As a primary background; as body text; in large filled areas
- **Emotion:** Luxury, warmth, refinement, celebration

### Secondary Colors

#### Névue Cream — `#EDE0CC`
The warm, linen-toned neutral. The IV bag, tote bag, and background walls all carry this warmth. It is not stark white — it has warmth and texture, evoking natural linen and canvas.

- **Use for:** Warm backgrounds, email backgrounds, card fills, secondary CTAs
- **Pairs with:** Névue Navy (primary pairing), Névue Gold (accent on cream)

#### Névue Linen — `#F5EDD8`
A lighter, airier version of Cream. The warm-lit room backgrounds in the IV suite photography. Used for large-area backgrounds where full cream feels too saturated.

- **Use for:** Page backgrounds, email wrappers, hero section backgrounds

#### Névue Marble — `#F2EFEC`
The cool, slightly veined white of the marble surfaces throughout the brand. Tray, door surround, counter surfaces. Slightly cooler than cream — this is the clean, clinical side of the palette.

- **Use for:** Cards and surfaces that need to feel clean rather than warm; medical/clinical contexts
- **Note:** When representing marble, pair with a subtle CSS gradient or texture if possible

### Utility Colors

#### Névue White — `#FAFAF8`
Near-white with just a breath of warmth. Never use pure `#FFFFFF` — it reads clinical and cold for this brand.

- **Use for:** Text on dark backgrounds, icon fills, negative space

#### Névue Charcoal — `#1A1A2E`
Deep charcoal with a navy undertone. Primary text color on light backgrounds. Softer than pure black, maintaining warmth.

- **Use for:** Body text, captions, footnotes, UI labels

#### Névue Mist — `#D6CFC5`
A mid-tone warm gray. Used for borders, dividers, disabled states, and placeholder text.

- **Use for:** Borders, dividers, secondary text, disabled states

### Color Combinations

**Premium (primary):** Névue Navy + Névue Gold + Névue White
- Navy background, gold accent details, white text
- Used for: hero sections, primary CTAs, packaging, signage

**Warm (editorial):** Névue Linen + Névue Navy + Névue Gold
- Linen background, navy type, gold accents
- Used for: email campaigns, editorial layouts, social content

**Clean (clinical):** Névue Marble + Névue Charcoal + Névue Gold
- Marble background, charcoal text, gold accents
- Used for: product pages, service descriptions, clinical content

**Reversed (highlight):** Névue Cream + Névue Navy text
- Cream background, navy type, gold line accents
- Used for: testimonials, pull quotes, feature callouts

## Typography

*See `fonts.md` for full font library, import code, and usage rules.*

### Font Stack Summary

| Role | Font | Weight | Style |
|------|------|--------|-------|
| Display / Wordmark | Cormorant Garamond | 600–700 | Normal, wide tracking |
| Headings (H1–H2) | Playfair Display | 700 | Normal |
| Subheadings (H3–H4) | Playfair Display | 500 | Normal or Italic |
| Body Text | Inter | 400 | Normal |
| UI / Functional | DM Sans | 400–500 | Normal |
| Pull Quotes / Accent | Cormorant Garamond | 400 | Italic |
| Labels / Metadata | DM Sans | 400 | Uppercase, tracked |

### Typographic Principles

1. **Space is luxury.** Use generous line height (1.6–1.8 for body, 1.2 for headlines). Tight, crowded type feels cheap.
2. **Track display text wide.** NÉVUE wordmark and headline text should use letter-spacing of 0.05–0.15em. Wide tracking signals refinement.
3. **Hierarchy is clear.** Never have two competing levels of similar visual weight on the same screen.
4. **Serif for emotion, sans for function.** Cormorant/Playfair for anything that needs to feel beautiful. Inter/DM Sans for anything that needs to be acted on quickly.
5. **Never use bold on body copy.** Use italic + color contrast for emphasis instead.

## Logo Usage

### Wordmark Rules
- Always use the é accent. NÉVUE, never NEVUE.
- Primary lockup: All caps, wide letter-spacing (0.15–0.25em), Cormorant Garamond Semibold
- Minimum clear space: The height of the letter "N" on all four sides
- Minimum size: 80px wide (digital), 1 inch wide (print)

### On backgrounds
| Background | Logo color |
|-----------|-----------|
| Névue Navy | Névue Gold or Névue White |
| Névue Cream / Linen | Névue Navy |
| Névue Marble | Névue Charcoal or Névue Navy |
| Photography (light) | Névue Navy |
| Photography (dark) | Névue White |

### Never
- Stretch, compress, or rotate the wordmark
- Use the wordmark in Névue Mist or any gray tone
- Place on a busy photographic background without an overlay
- Use drop shadows on the wordmark

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
NÉVUE speaks like a knowledgeable, warm friend who happens to be a board-certified physician. Not distant or clinical. Not over-friendly or salesy. Poised.

### Tone Principles

**Precise over vague**
- ✗ "We help you feel better"
- ✓ "Your Myers' Cocktail delivers direct-to-bloodstream hydration, B-complex, and magnesium — results you feel within the hour"

**Elevated over excited**
- ✗ "Amazing results!! 🎉 Book NOW!"
- ✓ "The results speak quietly. Clients return monthly."

**Confident over persuasive**
- ✗ "You NEED to try this"
- ✓ "When you're ready, we're here."

**Warm over formal**
- ✗ "Dear valued client, we are pleased to inform you..."
- ✓ "Your appointment is confirmed. We look forward to seeing you."

**Active over passive**
- ✗ "Results are experienced by most clients within 24 hours"
- ✓ "Most clients feel results within 24 hours"

### Words We Use
luminous · refined · restorative · precision · curated · elevated · effortless · transformative · timeless · atelier · bespoke · artisan · clinical · efficacy · radiance · revitalized · sculpted · tailored

### Words We Avoid
cheap · deal · discount · limited time only · amazing · incredible · awesome · you guys · basically · just · super · crazy (as in "crazy good") · affordable (implies price consciousness — use "accessible" if needed)

### Punctuation & Formatting
- Em dashes (—) over hyphens for pauses. No space before/after: "The results—and the experience—speak for themselves."
- Periods at the end of standalone sentences in CTAs. "Book Your Visit."
- Oxford comma always.
- Numbers: spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10+.
- Percent: "10%" not "10 percent" in headlines; either in body.
- Ellipses (…) are acceptable for trailing elegant pauses. Not for urgency.
- Never use multiple exclamation marks. One exclamation point is the absolute maximum per communication.

## Photography Style

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Color palette:** Warm cream, navy, gold, white, marble — all naturally present in the scenes
- **Lighting:** Soft, directional natural light. Warm golden hour tones. Never harsh flash or clinical fluorescent.
- **Composition:** Centered subjects, generous negative space, intentional styling
- **Props:** Marble surfaces, white orchids, champagne glasses, linen, brushed gold hardware, clean glass

### Subject Photography
- Models: Natural, confident, at ease. Not smiling with forced enthusiasm.
- Staff: Polished, precise, professional. Navy scrubs or white lab coats only.
- Products: On marble or linen surfaces. Clean, uncluttered. Single hero product per frame preferred.

### What to avoid
- Stock photography that looks generic
- Overly retouched or artificial images
- Backgrounds with busy patterns
- Neon, bright, or saturated color palettes
- Anything that reads as "discount medical spa"

## Design Principles

### 1. White Space is Intentional
Luxe brands breathe. Never fill every available pixel. The space around an element is as important as the element itself.

### 2. One Hero, Everything Else Supports
Each page, email, or social post should have one dominant visual element. Everything else recedes.

### 3. Restraint is the Point
When in doubt, do less. Remove. Simplify. The NÉVUE brand is defined by what is absent as much as what is present.

### 4. Grid Consistency
- Column grid: 12 columns (web), 4 columns (mobile)
- Gutter: 24px (web), 16px (mobile)
- Max content width: 1200px
- Generous padding: never less than 80px vertical padding on sections (web)

### 5. Borders and Lines
Use fine lines (1px) in Névue Gold or Névue Mist to divide sections — not thick bars or colored blocks.

## Email Design Standards

- Background: Névue Linen (`#F5EDD8`) or Névue Marble (`#F2EFEC`)
- Max width: 600px
- Header: NÉVUE wordmark in Névue Navy, centered, generous padding
- Body text: Inter 16px, Névue Charcoal, line-height 1.7
- CTA button: Névue Navy background, Névue White text, 0px border-radius (squared corners signal luxury — rounded corners feel friendly/casual)
- Footer: Small DM Sans text in Névue Mist; unsubscribe link always present

|----------|---------------|-----------|

| LinkedIn | 1.91:1 (1200×628) | 1:1 square |

| Pinterest | 2:3 (1000×1500) | — |

### Visual rules for social
- Always include the NÉVUE wordmark or a brand color anchor
- Text on image: Cormorant Garamond for display, DM Sans for functional text
- Emoji: Permitted sparingly; prefer ✨ 🤍 🌿 — never 🔥 💥 🚨
- Hashtag count: Instagram max 5 (quality over quantity); LinkedIn 3 max

## Agent Usage Instructions

**Every agent should:**
1. Read this file once per session before creating any branded output
2. Reference `brand-tokens.json` when specifying any color in a design brief or HTML/CSS output
3. Reference `fonts.md` when specifying typography in any creative brief, HTML template, or design direction
4. Apply the Voice & Tone section to ALL copy — emails, ads, social posts, sales decks, and landing pages
5. Never create branded output that uses colors, fonts, or language outside this guide without explicit approval

**Quick reference for copy agents (Agent 3):**
- Default to Cormorant Garamond for any headline in a design brief
- Wide tracking on display text always
- Warm, confident, precise — not excited
- Cream/Navy/Gold palette assumed unless stated otherwise

**Quick reference for paid/outbound agents (Agent 4):**
- Ad creative must use brand colors — no off-palette choices for "testing"
- Headlines: Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond in brief specs
- No discount language. No urgency tactics. Premium positioning always.

**Quick reference for email agents (Agent 5):**
- Linen background, navy/charcoal text, gold accents
- CTA buttons: squared corners, navy fill, white text
- Subject lines: sophisticated and specific — never "🚨 SALE 🚨"
