## What Is YesCoach

YesCoach is a **biomechanics-based movement health app** (Android, early access) that makes the invisible visible. It replaces generic workout trackers with a system that actually explains what your training is doing — which muscles are working, how recovered they are, and what patterns are emerging over time.

Current focus: strength training with biomechanics intelligence. Stretching (dynamic + static, per-muscle) exists but is not yet fully developed. The vision: the go-to app for your movement health — strength, mobility, recovery, eventually rehab and sport.

**Narrative control note:** Lead with strength tracking + intelligence layer (solid, differentiated). Hint at movement health direction without over-committing to stretching/mobility until that feature is polished. Roadmap can be accelerated if a marketing opportunity requires it.

Core tagline territory: *"See what your training is doing."* / *"Your movement health, understood."*

---

## The Problem It Solves

Most people who lift seriously are still flying blind:
- They follow programs without understanding why
- They don't know if the right muscles are actually firing
- They guess at recovery — push too hard, or leave gains on the table
- Plateaus feel random because there's no feedback loop
- Workout apps are dumb logs — they record, they don't explain

YesCoach closes that gap. It's not just tracking — it's **understanding**.

## Primary Target Audience

**The serious self-coached hobbyist lifter**
- Trains 3–5x/week
- No personal trainer, programs their own training
- Wants to understand their body, not just follow a template
- Intellectually curious about training — reads about exercise science, watches form breakdowns
- Frustrated that most apps treat them like a beginner with a checklist

**Broader vision:** Anyone who wants to understand and improve their movement health — strength training, mobility, recovery, and eventually rehab and sport performance. The muscle-first UX means even less experienced users can explore without being overwhelmed. This is the go-to app for how your body moves and recovers.

## Key Differentiators

### 1. Biomechanics Engine (not just labels)
Muscle activation is calculated from joint torque mechanics — not generic "this exercise works your chest" labels. Load intensity (% 1RM) changes the activation picture. This is research-accurate and unique in consumer apps.

### 2. Muscle-First Navigation
The UX is built around the body, not lists. Tap a muscle → see movement patterns → configure → add to workout. This minimizes decision fatigue and teaches users how exercises relate to their body. Competing apps (Hevy, Strong, Fitbod) are still list-based.

### 3. Intelligence Layer (Premium)
Week/month visual summaries — body map graphics showing volume distribution, muscle recovery status, training balance. You can "hold" a muscle map over time to see what you've been training. This is the smart tracking differentiator.

### 4. Recovery Visibility
See which muscles are recovered and which aren't. Plan sessions by readiness, not calendar. No other consumer app does this from a biomechanical modeling standpoint.

### 5. Adapts to Your History
Strength patterns in one movement inform others. Suggestions evolve as your training data grows. Foundation for AI-generated programming down the road.

## Business Model

| Tier | What's included |
|------|----------------|
| **Free** | Full workout logging, planning, day view, muscle-first navigation, exercise browsing |
| **Premium** | Intelligence layer: week/month visual summaries, body map analytics, muscle group tracking over time |

Monetization via RevenueCat (in-app subscription, localized pricing).

**Pricing:**
- Monthly: $2.99 CAD / ~$2.99 USD / ~799 INR
- Annual: $19.99 CAD / ~$19.99 USD / ~1900 INR
- Positioning: "a cup of coffee a month, a lunch a year" — impulse-buy monthly, commitment annual

**Current state:** One premium trial subscriber (India, serious lifter). They downloaded, explored the app, then converted to 1-month premium trial after a few clicks — strong in-app conversion signal. However, they haven't returned since — activation/retention gap. Public open beta on Google Play (pre-launch testing stage, not fully shipped).

**Beta engagement mechanic (planned):** Work out 3 times within 7 days + submit feedback → unlock premium free for a set period. Forces real activation, filters for serious users, generates product feedback, and rewards the right behavior. Only people who experience the app's core value get the reward. Smart growth loop.

## Roadmap Signal (for positioning)

- **Near-term:** Expand exercise library, refine biomechanics accuracy, improve onboarding
- **Mid-term:** AI/smart algo to generate workouts based on your own training data and goals
- **Long-term:** Rehab integration, granular muscle fiber tracking (fast twitch vs slow twitch), sport-specific training modes, movement quality assessment (computer vision), injury risk modeling

This is not a logger — it's a platform for training intelligence. The long-term vision spans fitness → rehab → sport performance.

## Founder & Team Profile

**Solo engineer-builder.** Not a certified trainer, but legitimately educated:
- Read NSCA textbooks (the gold standard of strength & conditioning science)
- Attended theoretical YMCA training (no practical cert — honest boundary)
- Fairly fit, actively uses the app

**Story angle:** Engineer frustrated by the lack of real data in fitness apps, built the tool they wished existed. Classic "scratch your own itch" — authentic and credible without overclaiming expertise.

**Content credibility:** Can speak to exercise science from a curious, rigorous learner perspective. Not "coach says" — "here's what the research actually shows." This is the right lane for the target audience (self-coached, analytically-minded lifters).

**Wife — tester + future asset:**
- Experienced female lifter + nutritionist
- Visibly fit — aspirational figure in the female fitness space
- First real tester of the product
- **Key feedback:** The app highlighted her lower body dominance, which made her feel bad — reminded her of when she trained more consistently. Currently has less time to work out than before.
- **Product insight from her feedback:** Data-honest design can feel like judgment for users who aren't currently training at their peak. The app's framing needs to feel like a neutral mirror ("here's your current pattern") not a scorecard ("here's what you're neglecting"). This is especially sensitive for returning or time-constrained users.
- **Note on nutrition:** YesCoach has no nutrition features — don't position around nutrition even with her background.
- **Marketing role:** Not the right moment to make her a public face — she's in a lower-activity phase and the app is surfacing that gap for her. Revisit when she's back in a consistent training rhythm.

## Early User Research

**Current testers:** Wife + close friend (serious lifter, home gym with squat rack, barbell, bench press).

**Friend's verdict:** Ready to go out, minor UX improvements aside. Specifically called out the need for better onboarding or video walkthroughs to understand the app's value quickly.

**Consistent signal:** Both the India subscriber (converted but didn't return) and the friend's feedback point to the same gap — the app works, but the *first session experience* doesn't yet communicate its value fast enough.

---

## Product Gaps (Marketing Relevant)

- **Onboarding/activation:** Consistent signal from early testers and first subscriber — first session doesn't deliver the "aha" moment quickly enough. Video or guided walkthrough needed.
- **Emotional framing of imbalance data:** Wife's feedback revealed the app can make users feel bad about gaps in their training (e.g. lower body dominance surfaced when she's less active than before). Data needs to feel like a neutral, empowering mirror — not a judgment. Copy and UI framing matters here.
- **iOS unavailable:** Blocks a large segment of the target audience. Web version is a workaround but not prominent.
- **No community layer:** No social proof, no user stories, no word-of-mouth loop yet.
- **Exercise library depth:** Still expanding — may limit perceived completeness for power users.
- **No AI programming yet:** The most compelling future feature isn't available — positioning must balance current value vs. roadmap without overpromising.
