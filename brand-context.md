## What SAWT Is (And Isn't)

### What Sawt IS

**Primary Definition:**
Sawt is an agent orchestration platform that automates and perfects customer interactions at enterprise scale.

**Secondary Explanation:**
We build AI agents that handle any interaction between a company and its customers—whether inbound or outbound, across voice, WhatsApp, email, web, and more. One agent, same memory, working everywhere.

**Business Model:**
We have two divisions:
- **Sawt Products:** Our platform—a complete product suite for customer interaction automation
- **Sawt Solutions:** Custom implementations for specific enterprise and government needs

**Technical Description:**
We're building the application layer of the AI space—specifically, any AI use case that involves interactive engagement with end users.

**Market Position:**
The agent orchestration platform that powers 90% of AI-enabled calls in Saudi Arabia.

### What Sawt is NOT

We are NOT:
- Just a voice AI company (despite our name meaning "voice")
- A call center replacement (we're an orchestration layer on top)
- A typical AI startup (we're an engineers-first company)
- Focused on individuals or small businesses (enterprise and government only)
- Promising "better customer experience" than humans (we're honest: we're faster and cheaper, not necessarily "better")

### Common Misconceptions to Correct

| Misconception | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| "You just do phone calls" | "We orchestrate agents across all channels" |
| "You're an AI chatbot company" | "We're a customer interaction automation platform" |
| "You replace call centers" | "We're an additional layer that optimizes and automates" |
| "You're a startup" | "We're the market leader processing millions of interactions monthly" |

## Core Philosophy

### The Big Idea: Commoditizing Expertise

**Core Thesis:**
"We turn expertise into a commodity. Companies pay millions for expert debt collectors or world-class customer service agents. We deliver that same expertise as software—at scale, with perfect consistency."

### How We Think About Our Business

#### 1. Use Case Perfection Over Platform Features
- We don't sell "a platform with features"
- We sell "perfected use cases with proven results"
- Every use case should have 5+ proven implementations

#### 2. Depth Over Breadth
- Better to own 3 use cases perfectly than 10 use cases poorly
- "We've done debt collection 7 times" is more powerful than "we can do anything"
- Each new client benefits from all previous implementations

#### 3. Case Study Driven Sales
- Every pitch should reference similar clients in the same use case
- Show metrics, not promises
- Recycle and build on previous work

#### 4. Technical Excellence First
- We are an engineers-first company
- Our competitive advantage is our technical team
- Engineering depth should be visible in everything we do

#### 5. Honest About Trade-offs
- We're faster and cheaper, not necessarily "better experience"
- We optimize for speed, cost, and scale—not warmth
- We solve problems faster and more concisely

## Brand Voice & Personality

### How Sawt Should Sound

**We are:**
- Technical but accessible (like Anthropic)
- Sophisticated but not pretentious
- Confident but not arrogant
- Honest about trade-offs
- Data-driven with proof to back claims
- Engineers-first in tone and substance

**We are NOT:**
- Startup-y or "scrappy"
- Over-hyped or using marketing fluff
- Making promises we can't keep
- Copying local competitors
- Generic or vague

### Writing Style Guidelines

**DO:**
- Lead with data and metrics
- Use charts, graphs, and visualizations
- Show technical depth
- Reference specific implementations
- Be honest about limitations
- Write like you're educating, not selling
- Use precise language

**DON'T:**
- Use buzzwords without substance ("revolutionary," "game-changing")
- Make vague claims ("better experience" without proof)
- Over-promise on customer satisfaction
- Use exclamation marks excessively
- Write fluffy marketing copy
- Ignore the technical audience

### Content Format Preferences

**Style Inspiration:** Anthropic

**Preferred Formats:**
- Articles with charts and benchmarks
- Visual examples over heavy text
- Technical diagrams and architecture
- Case studies with real metrics
- Data visualizations
- Video demos showing real product

**Avoid:**
- Text-heavy articles without visuals
- Generic stock photos
- Vague infographics
- Testimonials without metrics
- "Startup aesthetic" graphics

## Approved & Prohibited Messaging

### APPROVED MESSAGING

| DO SAY | WHY |
|--------|-----|
| "We perfect use cases through proven implementations" | Emphasizes depth and experience |
| "90% of AI calls in KSA run through Sawt" | Credible, verifiable, impressive |
| "Half the cost with 99.5% uptime" | Honest value prop with proof |
| "We've done debt collection 7 times—we know it perfectly" | Specific, credible, differentiating |
| "Faster resolution, zero wait times, consistent quality" | Honest benefits we can deliver |
| "Built by engineers from Berkeley, Stanford, Yale" | Technical credibility |
| "Trusted by Tabby, Floward, and government entities" | Social proof from real clients |
| "We commoditize expertise" | Unique positioning, thought-provoking |
| "Agent orchestration platform" | Category ownership |
| "Engineers-first company" | Brand positioning |

### PROHIBITED MESSAGING

| DON'T SAY | WHY NOT |
|-----------|---------|
| "Better customer experience than humans" | Not credible, we've admitted this isn't true |
| "Revolutionary" or "game-changing" | Generic startup buzzwords |
| "We replace your call center" | Not accurate—we're a layer on top |
| "Just as good as a human agent" | Sets wrong expectation |
| "Cheap automation" | Hurts brand, lowers negotiating power |
| "AI-powered call center" | Too narrow, misses orchestration |
| "We do everything" | Dilutes our depth positioning |
| "Startup disrupting X industry" | Conflicts with "established leader" brand |
| "Perfect customer satisfaction" | Can't deliver, hurts credibility |

### Reframing Guide

When tempted to say weak messaging, use strong messaging instead:

| Weak Messaging | Strong Messaging |
|----------------|------------------|
| "Better experience" | "Faster resolution, zero wait times" |
| "AI-powered" | "Agent orchestration platform" |
| "We're innovative" | "We've perfected this across 7 implementations" |
| "Best in market" | "90% market share in KSA" |
| "Revolutionary technology" | "Battle-tested at enterprise scale" |
| "Easy to use" | "Integrates with your existing systems" |
| "Affordable" | "Half the cost with proven ROI" |
| "Growing startup" | "Market leader processing millions of interactions" |

## Quick Reference Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

### Brand Check
- [ ] Does this feel like an "engineers' company"?
- [ ] Is it sophisticated, not startup-y?
- [ ] Are we showing depth, not just breadth?
- [ ] Is there data/proof to back claims?

### Messaging Check
- [ ] Are we leading with use case perfection?
- [ ] Are we being honest about trade-offs?
- [ ] Are we avoiding marketing fluff?
- [ ] Are we showing technical excellence?

### Visual Check
- [ ] Does it look like Anthropic/Browser Company quality?
- [ ] Are we using real product, not stock images?
- [ ] Is there data visualization or technical depth?
- [ ] Does it feel established, not scrappy?

### Audience Check
- [ ] Would an engineer find this credible?
- [ ] Would a CFO see clear ROI?
- [ ] Would a COO trust our operations?
- [ ] Are we speaking to enterprises, not individuals?

### Decision Framework

**If you're ever unsure about messaging, positioning, or brand voice, ask yourself:**

> "Would this make the 10 smartest people I know think 'This is actually very cool'?"

- If yes → proceed
- If no → refine until it does
