# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-12*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Where anglers connect and master the waters together.
**What it does:** FishAngler transforms logged catch data into personalized insights. It learns from each log — conditions, location, species — to drive progress and self-mastery. It surfaces guidance that improves decision-making before and during trips, and connects anglers through shared experiences, progress, and fishing stories.
**Product category:** Recreational Sports > Fishing / Outdoors
**Product type:** B2C Mobile App (primary) + Web App (secondary)
**Business model:** Membership subscription (monthly/annual) + B2B Sponsored Activation & Engagement Programs

## Brand Positioning
**Our Why:** FishAngler exists to fuel the endless pursuit of outdoor adventure through epic fishing. Every cast, every trip, every fish tells a story — and helps anglers experience more of those moments, not by luck, but through deeper understanding.
**Vision:** Become the go-to guide to every fishing adventure.
**Core pillars:**
1. **Insight** — a data model that creates practical intel and personalized guidance
2. **Guidance** — a user experience that reveals intel and helps users fish smarter
3. **Progress** — gamified systems that reward log consistency, learning, and mastery
4. **Connection** — social mechanics that link anglers by performance and passion

## Target Audience
**Primary user:** Weekend anglers who want to level up their skills — students, working adults, and family-oriented people with limited time to fish
**Geography:** US-centric
**Primary use case:** Finding *when* to go (fishing forecast/conditions) and *how* to fish (technique and guidance)
**Jobs to be done:**
- Stop wasting precious trips and start catching fish consistently
- Understand why fish bite in certain conditions and apply that knowledge
- Track progress and see real improvement over time
- Connect with other anglers who share the same passion

## User Archetypes
Each archetype is a lens, not a box. The best product moments intersect all three.

| Archetype | Motivation | What They Seek |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| **The Explorer** | Adventure and the unknown | New waters, species, and other anglers' experiences |
| **The Student** | Understanding the "why" | Techniques, guidance, and insights to refine their craft |
| **The Competitor** | Improvement and achievement | Stats, streaks, and an edge over their personal best |

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Anglers have limited time — every trip counts. The #1 frustration is wasting time and money looking for fish instead of catching them.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- YouTube and forums are fragmented and generic — no personalization
- Competing mobile apps are feature farms with no intelligent guidance or real camaraderie
- None connect real-time conditions + technique + personal history in one place
**What it costs them:** Wasted time, wasted money, and the emotional sting of coming home empty-handed
**Emotional tension:** Frustration, self-doubt, and the nagging feeling that others know something they don't

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct (apps):** Fishbrain, Fishbox, Deep Dive, OnX Fish, OnWater Fish, Bass Forecast, Windy.app, Fishing Points — fall short because they are feature farms with no intelligent guidance or real angler camaraderie built in
**Direct (membership):** SaltStrong — niche (inshore saltwater only), no broader angler community
**Indirect:** ChatGPT — generic AI with no fishing-specific data model or personalization from logged catch history

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't want to log every catch" | Even minimal logging unlocks FINN™ insights — every data point makes your guidance more personal |
| "The subscription is too much" | One better trip pays for a year. FINN™ gameplans save wasted time and money on unproductive outings |
| "I already know where I want to go" | Knowing your spots is step one — FINN™ tells you *when* to go and *how* to fish them for maximum success |
| "I don't want to share my personal spots" | Spot privacy controls are built in. You share only what you choose |

**Anti-persona:** The "know-it-all" angler with no interest in learning or improving; the professional fishing guide (already has the expertise and client relationships to protect)

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Wasted trips and repeated lack of success — one too many blank outings with nothing to show for it
**Pull:** FINN™ real-time Gameplans — "like having a guide right there with you" before and during every trip
**Habit:** Years of catch history locked in another app; logging feels too time-consuming to restart
**Anxiety:** "Will the data actually be accurate for my local spots?" / "Will it eat up time I could spend actually fishing?"

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "When's the best time to go fishing for [species]?"
- "What do [species] bite in these conditions?"
- "What's the conditions at my spot next weekend?"
- "What's the fish forecast look like this week?"

**How they describe FishAngler today (not aspirational):**
- "A fishing app that helps you find spots and see catches"

**Aspirational description (what we're building toward):**
- "My personal fishing guide — it knows my waters, my history, and tells me exactly when and how to fish"

**Words to use:** Guide, mastery, smarter, personalized, gameplan, intel, forecast, progress, challenge, community
**Words to avoid:** Generic outdoor/adventure language; overpromising catches ("guarantee," "always catch fish"); tech jargon that excludes casual anglers

## Brand Voice
**Tone:** Knowledgeable but accessible — expert without being pretentious
**Style:** Motivational local fishing guide meets data-informed coach; adapts by context:
- Educational content → Authoritative, clear, helpful
- Celebration content → Enthusiastic, congratulatory, inspiring
- Product/feature content → Exciting, benefit-focused, practical
- Community content → Warm, inclusive, authentic

**Personality:** Motivational · Community-focused · Action-oriented · Data-informed · Authentic

**Do's:**
- Use fishing-specific terminology naturally — anglers know their craft
- Lead with benefits, not features
- Emphasize progress, growth, and achievement
- Highlight community and connection
- Use active voice and strong verbs

**Don'ts:**
- Never overpromise catches — fishing success always varies
- Don't use jargon that excludes casual anglers
- Don't make fishing sound "solved" — respect the challenge and the pursuit
- Avoid generic outdoor/adventure language — be fishing-specific
